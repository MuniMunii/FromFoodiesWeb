Ingredients

Taiyaki

  • 170 grams butter (6 ounces, 12 tablespoons)
  • 65 grams light brown sugar (2.3 ounces, 8 tablespoons)
  • 100 grams egg (2 eggs, 3.5 ounces)
  • 240 grams cake flour (8.5 ounces, 1 1/2 cup)
  • 6 grams baking powder (0.2 ounces, 2 teaspoons)
  • 155 grams warm milk (5.5 ounces, 2/3 cup)
  • 10 grams honey (0.35 ounces, 2 teaspoons)
  • 6 grams light soy sauce (0.2 ounces, 2 teaspoons)
  • 4 grams vanilla extract (0.14 ounces, 2 teaspoons)

RED BEAN PASTE (OPTIONAL)

  • 3 parts sugar / 150 grams (5.3 ounces)
  • 5 parts red beans / 250 grams (8.8 ounces)
  • 10 parts water / 500 grams (17.6 ounces)

Story Behind Taiyaki

Taiyaki was first sold in Japan in 1909. It is essentially a reshaped form of imagawayaki, an already popular snack made by wrapping bean paste in flour skin.

Seijirō Kobe, founder of the store Naniwaya Sōhonten (浪花家総本店), was having trouble selling his imagawayaki, so he decided to bake the cakes into fish shapes resembling tai, or red sea bream.

How To Make

Taiyaki

  1. Pressure cook the adzuki beans with the water for 1 hour.
  2. If you are not using a pressure cooker, you can cook in a covered saucepan for 1 1/2 hours, topping off the water as it cooks off. The beans are ready when they can be crushed easily between your fingers. Strain off any excess water.
  3. Purée the beans in a food processor until you achieve a fine, sandy texture. It will seem very dry, but it will be rehydrated in the next step.
  4. Return the beans to a saucepan and add just enough water until the bean paste barely starts to clump together.
  5. Adding the sugar will finish bringing together the paste so don't feel pressured to add water to make it completely smooth.
  6. Cook the beans with the sugar, stirring until the paste comes together and the sugar fully dissolves into the paste, about 3 minutes.
  7. Allow the paste to cool until you can comfortably handle it with your hands..
  8. Pass the beans through a sieve to remove any chunks for a finer paste.
  9. Shape the paste into inserts that will fit inside the fish-shaped taiyaki.

Prepare the Taiyaki

  1. Mix together the milk, honey, soy sauce, and vanilla extract and set aside.
  2. Cream the butter together with the sugar
  3. Beat in one egg, then the other
  4. Beat in half the flour, then the milk, then the other half of flour
  5. The batter should form a fluid—but not liquid—batter. It should be about the consistency of soft serve ice cream. Set your burner to between low and medium-low and begin to heat your taiyaki pan.
  6. Use a squeeze bottle to squeeze a pool of batter into the body, then the tail, and around the body again
  7. Place the red bean insert into the center of the taiyaki and press in halfway.
  8. Pour more batter over the red bean insert so that it's fully covered.
  9. Check the doneness of each side and if not done, continue grilling at 45-second intervals.
  10. The taiyaki are done when they are evenly golden brown. Remove with a pair of chopsticks.
  11. Serve while still warm and crunchy on the outside. Oishi!
