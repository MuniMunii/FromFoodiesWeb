Ingredients

Pudding a la Mode

  • 1 package of instant pudding mix
  • 2 cups of milk
  • 1 cup of heavy cream
  • 2 tablespoons of sugar
  • 1 teaspoon of vanilla extract
  • 1 pint of ice cream
  • Whipped cream (optional)
  • Chocolate syrup (optional)

Story Behind Pudding a la Mode

Pudding a la mode is a dessert that originated in France and is commonly served in American restaurants. The term "a la mode" means "in the fashion" or "in the style" in French. In the context of desserts, it typically refers to a dish served with a scoop of vanilla ice cream on top. The origins of pudding a la mode are not clear, but it is believed to have been popularized in the United States in the late 19th century. It was a popular dessert during the Victorian era, when elaborate and luxurious dishes were fashionable. Today, pudding a la mode is a classic dessert that can be found on the menu of many American restaurants. It is typically made with a creamy vanilla pudding or custard, topped with a scoop of vanilla ice cream and drizzled with caramel or chocolate sauce. The combination of warm pudding and cold ice cream creates a delicious contrast in temperature and texture. Pudding a la mode can be made in many variations, including using different types of pudding flavors such as chocolate, butterscotch, or banana. It can also be served with toppings such as whipped cream, chopped nuts, or fresh fruit.

How To Make

Pudding a la Mode

  1. In a mixing bowl, combine the instant pudding mix and 2 cups of milk. Whisk together for 2 minutes, then set aside to thicken.
  2. In a separate mixing bowl, beat the heavy cream, sugar, and vanilla extract together until soft peaks form
    3. .
  3. Add the whipped cream mixture to the pudding mixture and fold together gently until well combined.
  4. Spoon the pudding mixture into serving dishes and chill for at least 30 minutes.
  5. When ready to serve, add a scoop of ice cream to each dish and top with whipped cream and chocolate syrup, if desired. Enjoy!
