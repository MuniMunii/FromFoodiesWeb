Ingredients

Daifuku

  • 150 grams glutinous rice flour
  • 2 tablespoons sugar
  • 2 tablespoons sugar
  • Potato starch or cornstarch
  • Red bean paste, strawberry jam, or other fillings of your choice
  • Optional: food coloring, kinako (toasted soybean flour), matcha (green tea powder), or other toppings

Story Behind Daifuku

Daifuku is a traditional Japanese confectionery that is widely enjoyed in Japan and around the world. It is a soft, round mochi (glutinous rice cake) filled with sweet red bean paste (anko) or other fillings such as strawberries, chestnuts, or green tea. Daifuku has a long history dating back to the 18th century in Kyoto, Japan. It was originally a luxury item enjoyed by the upper classes, but eventually became more widely available and popular among the general population. The name "daifuku" is derived from the Japanese words "dai" meaning "great" and "fuku" meaning "luck." It is said that eating daifuku brings good luck and fortune to those who consume it. Today, daifuku is enjoyed in many different variations, with different types of fillings and flavors. It is a popular snack and dessert in Japan, and has also gained popularity in other parts of the world.

How To Make

Daifuku

  1. In a mixing bowl, combine the glutinous rice flour and sugar.
  2. Gradually add water to the mixture, stirring constantly until it forms a smooth and sticky dough.
  3. If you want to add food coloring, divide the dough into portions and knead in the color until evenly distributed.
  4. Dust your work surface with potato starch or cornstarch. Take a portion of the dough and flatten it into a disc about 1/4 inch thick.
  5. Place a small amount of filling in the center of the disc. Fold the edges of the dough up and around the filling, pinching them together to seal.
  6. Roll the ball in your hands to make it smooth. Dust with potato starch or cornstarch to prevent sticking.
  7. Repeat with the remaining dough and filling.
  8. If desired, roll the daifuku in kinako or matcha powder to coat.
